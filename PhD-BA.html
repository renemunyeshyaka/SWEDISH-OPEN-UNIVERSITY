1. Ph.D. program in Business Administration

  • Title: Ph.D.
  • Level: Post-graduate, 3rd cycle
  • Language: English
  • Admissions: On call for applications
  • Duration: 4-6 years, 240 credits
  • Fees: 6,000 USD per year
  • Classes: Online/remote/seminars

Overview

The doctoral program in business administration at the Swedish Open University is a rigorous and comprehensive program designed for individuals seeking to pursue advanced research in business administration. The program provides an understanding of the latest theories and practices in the field of business administration and equips students with advanced research skills. Students will learn to conduct independent research, analyze data, and present their findings in a compelling manner. The curriculum of the doctoral program in business administration at the Swedish Open University covers a wide range of topics depending on the specialization. The program is designed to provide students with a strong foundation in business theory and research methods and to allow them to specialize in a particular area of business research.

The curriculum of a doctoral program in business administration is structured to provide students with a foundation in various business disciplines. Students are exposed to advanced theoretical frameworks, analytical tools, and empirical research methods that enable them to critically evaluate and contribute to the existing body of knowledge in their field.

The coursework for the PhD program in business administration comprises of foundation courses and specialization courses.

The foundation courses are:

• Quantitative Research Methods (15 credits).
• Qualitative Research Methods (15 credits): These courses provide students with a strong foundation in research methodologies and techniques relevant for research in business administration. Students will learn quantitative and qualitative research methods, data analysis techniques, and academic writing skills.
• Research Writing Skills in Business (15 credits): This course explores various theoretical frameworks and perspectives that underpin research in business administration. Students will examine theories related to organizations and other areas.

The specialization courses will be listed under the respective programs.

One of the central components of a doctoral program in business administration is the dissertation. A dissertation is a substantial research project that represents the culmination of a student's doctoral studies. It requires students to identify a significant research question, develop a theoretical framework, collect and analyze data, and present original findings that contribute to the knowledge and understanding of their chosen area of study.

The program is highly customizable, allowing students to specialize in areas of their interest. Students can specialize in areas such as business digitalization, sustainability, responsible management education, entrepreneurship, and organizational leadership.
