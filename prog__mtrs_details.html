MSc in Global Management

  • Title: Degree of Master of Science in Global Management
  • Level: Post-graduate
  • Language: English
  • Application deadline: 3 months before the semester starts.
  • Duration: 2 years (4 semesters), 120 credits
  • Fees: 3,500 USD per semester
  • Classes: Online/Remote

Overview

In today's world, businesses are increasingly operating across borders and there is a growing demand for professionals who understand the intricacies of conducting businesses in different cultural, political, and economic contexts. The global management program at the Swedish Open University prepares students to tackle these challenges and develop strategies for success. It is an excellent choice for students who are interested in advancing their careers in global business management. The program is designed to provide students with the knowledge and skills necessary to succeed in a global business environment while also promoting ethical and responsible practices. It focuses on developing a comprehensive understanding of the complexities of global business management, including cross-cultural communication, international trade, and strategic planning.

The online program allows students to study at their own pace following their own schedule, making it an excellent choice for busy professionals. On completion of the program, graduates will be well-equipped to pursue a range of careers in global management, including positions such as global business managers, international marketing directors, global supply chain managers, international consulting, and global business development. Graduates may also choose to pursue further studies like a PhD in global management or related fields.


Program Learning Outcomes and Objectives

On completion of the program, students will be able to:

• Understand the challenges and opportunities of global businesses.
• Analyze global markets and develop global strategies.
• Demonstrate a thorough understanding of global business management principles and practices.
• Analyze and evaluate the impact of cultural, economic, and political factors on global business operations.
• Develop and implement effective strategies for managing global teams and projects.
• Manage and lead global teams in organizations.
• Evaluate and apply ethical and sustainable practices in global business contexts.
• Communicate effectively with stakeholders across cultures and in different global business contexts.
• Apply analytical and critical thinking skills to solve complex business problems in global settings.

Program Structure

The master's program in global management is a 2-year program that consists of 60 credits for each year. The program is delivered online, allowing students to study at their own pace following their own schedule.

In the first year of the program, students will take core courses that cover the foundations of global management, including cross-cultural communication and negotiations, global strategies and operations, and international business law and ethics. They will also learn about global marketing and supply chain management, explore the latest tools and techniques for managing global supply chains, and market products and services to global consumers.

In the second year of the program, students will take elective courses that allow them to specialize in areas such as global finance, international entrepreneurship, and sustainable businesses. They will also have an opportunity to work on a project, applying their knowledge and skills to solve a real-world global management problem. The program includes the following courses:

1. International Financial Management (7.5)
2. Marketing Management (7.5)
3. Operations Management (7.5)
4. Supply Chain and Sustainable Business Practices (7.5)
5. Research Methods (7.5)
6. Strategic Management (7.5)
7. Project Management (7.5)
8. Human Resource Management (7.5)
9. Global Business Environment and Ethics (7.5)
10. Cross-Cultural Management (7.5)
11. Global Business Strategy (7.5)
12. Global Negotiations (7.5)
13. Global Entrepreneurship (7.5)
14. Organizational Behavior (7.5)

Thesis or Research Project (15 credits)

The thesis or research project is the culminating experience of the program. Students will work with a faculty advisor to design and execute a research project in global management. The project will be presented to a panel of experts who will evaluate it and provide feedback.

Career Opportunities

Graduates will be well-equipped to pursue careers in global business management, such as international business development, global marketing, global supply chain management, and consulting. They will have the skills to analyze and evaluate international business opportunities, develop and implement global business strategies, and communicate effectively with stakeholders from different cultural backgrounds.

Graduates can pursue a range of careers related to global business management, including:

• Global Business Managers • International Business Consultants • Global Marketing Managers • International Business Development Managers • Global Operations Managers • International HR Manager

Admission Requirements

To be considered for admission to the master’s program at the Swedish Open University, applicants must meet the following requirements:

• Hold a bachelor's degree from an accredited institution.
• Have a minimum GPA or equivalent of 70 percent.
• Provide evidence of proficiency in the English language.
• Submit a statement of purpose and a strong interest in the program.
• Complete an interview with the program faculty.
• Have a minimum of two years of professional work experience.

Tuition and Fees

The tuition fee for the master’s program at the Swedish Open University is 3,500 USD per semester. This does not include additional fees such as textbooks, technology fees, and other course material.

Assessment and evaluation

Student learning will be assessed using a number of methods, including exams, essays, case studies, and projects. Students will also be evaluated on their participation in online discussions and group projects. Course evaluations will be conducted at the end of each course to gather feedback from the students on the effectiveness of the course and the teaching methods used.

Graduation requirements

To graduate from the Bachelor’s Program in at Swedish Open University, students must:

• Complete all required courses and earn a minimum of 60 credits for a 1-year program. • Maintain a minimum grade of 60 percent in each course. • Successfully complete a master’s thesis or project. • Meet any additional requirements set by the university.
