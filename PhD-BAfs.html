1.2. Ph.D. program in Business Administration with a focus on sustainability

  • Title: Ph.D.
  • Level: Post-graduate, 3rd cycle
  • Language: English
  • Admissions: On call for applications
  • Duration: 4-6 years, 240 credits
  • Fees: 6,000 USD per year
  • Classes: Online/remote/seminars

Overview

A doctoral program in business administration with a focus on sustainability is an advanced academic program designed to equip doctoral candidates with the knowledge, skills, and tools needed for addressing the complex sustainability challenges facing businesses and society today.

The research component of a doctoral program in business administration with a focus on sustainability is typically a dissertation project that addresses a sustainability-related research question. This project requires students to conduct original research, analyze data, and develop insights and recommendations for addressing sustainability challenges in businesses and society. Students are expected to work closely with their dissertation advisors and committee members and present their findings in a written dissertation that meets rigorous academic standards.

The program aims to prepare students to:

• Develop an in-depth understanding of sustainability and its applications in organizations.
• Analyze and evaluate the impact of sustainability on organizational performance and outcomes.
• Understand the role of sustainability in creating and sustaining a healthy environment and society.
• Conduct original research in the field of sustainability.
• Communicate the research findings effectively to both academic and practitioner audiences.
• Contribute to the development of theories and models related to sustainability.

Besides the foundation courses, specialization courses for the PhD program in business administration with a focus on sustainability are:

• Sustainable Business Theory (15 credits): This course provides an in-depth exploration of sustainability frameworks, concepts, and principles in the context of business administration. Students will examine topics such as sustainable development, environmental management, social responsibility, and ethical decision-making.
• Sustainable Innovation and Entrepreneurship (15 credits): This course focuses on the role of innovation and entrepreneurship in driving sustainable business practices. Students will explore topics such as sustainable product development, circular economy, and social innovations. Additionally, the courses may cover areas such as sustainable supply chain management, renewable energy, sustainable finance, and corporate sustainability reporting.
